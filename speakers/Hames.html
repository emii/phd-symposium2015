Dr. Irene Hames

Research publication and peer review specialist

Irene Hames is an independent research-publication, -integrity and peer-review specialist with a PhD in cell biology and over 30 years’ experience in publishing. For 20 years, Irene was the managing editor of a large international science journal. She was a Council Member, Director and Trustee of COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics), taking on the role of Editor of COPE’s newsletters. She also originated the COPE Case Taxonomy and produced the COPE Ethical Guidelines for Peer Reviewers. In 2011 she was the specialist advisor to the UK Parliament for its inquiry into peer review. She is the author of the book Peer Review and Manuscript Management in Scientific Journals. Currently she runs workshops on research integrity and publication ethics, and holds advisory roles with Sense About Science, and Technical Editors (of which she was a founding Board member), and Axios Review. In December 2011 she was made a Fellow of the Society of Biology, and now sits on the Society’s Research Dissemination Committee.
